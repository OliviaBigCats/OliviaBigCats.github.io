what are big cats?

big cats fall under the felidae family, which is made up of 38 different species of cats. but only seven of them are big cats. lion, tiger, leopard, cheetah, jaguar, cougar and snow leopard.

Lions (Panthera Leo)

Conservation status: Vulnerable (Population decreasing)

Lifespan: 10 – 14 years (Adult, In the wild)

Diet: Carnivore (antelopes, buffaloes, zebras, young elephants, rhinos, hippos, wild hogs, crocodiles and giraffes. But they also sometimes eat smaller prey like mice, birds, hares, lizards, and tortoises.)

Habitat: south and eastern Africa and south Sahara desert and a small pride live sin India but they used to live in Europe, middle east, and northern India

quick facts:

the darker a lion's mane the older he is.

Only about 1 in 8 male lions survive to adulthood. A majority of lions die shortly after being kicked out of their pride around the age of 2.

Lions hunt mostly at night and have about a 50% success rate.

In Swahili, a lion is called simba.

A lion’s roar can be heard from as far as 5 miles away.

Lions have retractable claws, which mean that they could "hide" their claws during the play and avoid hurting each other.

Male African lions that are trying to take over a pride will kill all of the cubs to avoid competition.

Lions hunt in a pride that will have up to three males, around a dozen females, and their young
